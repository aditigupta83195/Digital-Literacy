Introduction

This project was assigned as part of the Digital Literacy course, where I took on the role of a Student Digital Ambassador. The main aim of this project was to understand the importance of digital skills in today’s world and help others become more aware of them.
Through this project, I worked on different tasks such as creating an infographic, building a digital portfolio, exploring online platforms, learning professional communication, and understanding cybercrime. Each task helped me gain practical knowledge about how to use digital tools effectively and responsibly.
Overall, this project helped me improve my awareness of online safety, communication, and professional presence, which are important for both academic and future career growth.

Task 1 – Digital Literacy Infographic

I created my digital literacy infographic using Canva because it is simple to use and offers many design templates and features. It helped me create a clean and visually appealing design without needing advanced skills.
In my infographic, I explained what digital literacy means and why it is important for students. I focused on three main areas: understanding digital literacy, safe internet practices, and email etiquette. I included simple tips like using strong passwords, avoiding suspicious links, and writing clear and respectful emails. I also used icons and colors to make the content more engaging and easy to understand.
While making this infographic, I found it interesting how design plays a big role in how people understand information. One challenge I faced was fitting all the information into one page without making it look crowded. This helped me learn how to keep content simple and focus on the most important points.

Task 4 – Email Etiquette

One situation where poor digital communication can cause problems is during group projects. For example, one team member shared assignment instructions in the group chat but did not clearly mention the deadline or divide the tasks properly. Because of this, some members misunderstood the work and others delayed their part, thinking they had more time. This led to confusion and last-minute stress, affecting the final submission.
This problem could have been avoided with clearer communication. The instructions should have been properly structured with clear deadlines and task division. Also, confirming that everyone understood their role would have helped avoid confusion. This shows how important clear and effective digital communication is for teamwork.

Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness

While working on the cybercrime task, I was surprised by how common online threats are in everyday student life. I realized that even simple actions like clicking on unknown links or sharing personal details can lead to serious problems. I used to think cybercrime mainly targeted large organizations, but now I understand that students are also easy targets.
What shocked me the most was how quickly online scams, especially UPI frauds, can happen and how difficult it is to recover lost money.
As a result, one habit I will change is being more careful while using the internet. I will avoid clicking on suspicious links, double-check payment requests, and never share sensitive information like OTPs. This task made me more aware and responsible about online safety.
